# Report Contract

Every generated report must include these sections.

## 1. Result Overview

Required fields:

- archetype name
- one-sentence pattern summary
- top 3 dominant tags translated into readable traits
- primary axis result
- main contradiction
- deep motive
- confidence band

Example:

```text
Archetype: The Observer Strategist
Pattern: You reduce uncertainty through observation, analysis, and controlled timing.
Dominant traits: analytical control, social distance, strategic patience.
Main contradiction: curiosity filtered through caution.
Deep motive: knowledge as control.
Confidence: high internal consistency.
```

## 2. Axis Cards

Six cards:

1. Exploration vs Safety-Control
2. Thinking Style
3. Relationship and Distance Pattern
4. Agency and Control
5. Ambiguity/Fear Response
6. Deep Motive

Each card must include:

- band
- explanation
- supporting evidence
- behavioral implication

## 3. Contradiction Map

Minimum: 1 contradiction  
Ideal: 2–3 contradictions  
Maximum: 4 contradictions

Never output more than 4 contradictions.

## 4. Strengths

Use concrete strengths, not flattery.

Good examples:

- pattern recognition
- emotional containment
- strategic patience
- controlled curiosity
- social reading
- fast execution
- independent judgment
- protective responsibility

Bad examples:

- you are special
- you are deep
- you are powerful
- you are different from everyone

## 5. Failure Modes

Every report must include failure modes.

Examples:

- over-observation
- delayed execution
- emotional distance
- over-control
- external validation dependency
- avoidant calmness
- risk underestimation
- missed opportunity through caution

## 6. Disproven-if Section

Every report must include at least one falsifier.

Example:

> This reading is weaker if you chose answers mainly because they sounded aesthetic rather than because they reflected your instinctive self-recognition.

Another:

> This reading is weaker if your answers reflect what you admire in fiction, not how you behave under real pressure.

## 7. Language rule

The report may be written in English, Arabic, Tunisian dialect, French, or German later.

The scoring engine must remain language-independent.
