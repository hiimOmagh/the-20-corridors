# Contradiction Rules

Each contradiction must produce:

1. name
2. detection rule
3. explanation
4. evidence cluster
5. behavioral implication
6. disproven-if condition

## C1 — Controlled Explorer

Detection:

```text
High EXP + high SAF/CTRL/OBS
```

Interpretation:

> The person is curious, but not blindly impulsive. They want discovery with control.

Behavioral implication:

> They may pursue new experiences after creating a safety or information buffer.

Disproven if:

> Risk/direct action dominates safety and observation.

## C2 — Solitary Leader

Detection:

```text
High LEAD/CTRL + high IND + low/moderate SOC
```

Interpretation:

> The person can lead, but does not necessarily seek closeness or group dependence.

Behavioral implication:

> Leadership is functional, not necessarily social or attention-seeking.

Disproven if:

> Social/cooperative tags are stronger than independence.

## C3 — Social Watcher

Detection:

```text
High SOC + high OBS/ANA
```

Interpretation:

> The person is people-oriented, but not naïvely open. They observe social dynamics before entering fully.

Behavioral implication:

> Strong potential for social intelligence, persuasion, or cautious intimacy.

Disproven if:

> Social answers are isolated and not repeated.

## C4 — Knowledge Without Action

Detection:

```text
High MEAN/ANA + low ACT/RISK
```

Interpretation:

> The person values knowledge, truth, or depth, but may not convert insight into action quickly.

Behavioral implication:

> Strong reflective intelligence, possible execution drag.

Disproven if:

> Direct action appears strongly in Q7, Q11, Q16, and Q15.

## C5 — Power Without Exposure

Detection:

```text
High CTRL/LEAD/REC + high AVD/SAF + low RISK
```

Interpretation:

> The person wants influence or control, but prefers low-exposure paths.

Behavioral implication:

> Strategic indirect control, possible reluctance to confront directly.

Disproven if:

> Risk and direct action are also high.

## C6 — Calm Avoider

Detection:

```text
High CALM + high AVD + low ACT
```

Interpretation:

> What looks like calmness may sometimes function as disengagement.

Behavioral implication:

> The person may avoid conflict without appearing anxious.

Disproven if:

> Calm choices are paired with responsibility and direct action.

## C7 — Recognition vs Independence Split

Detection:

```text
High REC + high IND
```

Interpretation:

> The person may want to be recognized, but not absorbed or controlled by the group.

Behavioral implication:

> Strong need for selective visibility: "see me, but don't own me."

Disproven if:

> Social closeness is consistently high and independence is weak.

## C8 — Responsible Avoider

Detection:

```text
High RESP + high AVD/SAF + low RISK
```

Interpretation:

> The person avoids direct exposure but still cares about correct or protective action.

Behavioral implication:

> They may warn, delegate, organize, or protect instead of personally entering danger.

Disproven if:

> Responsibility appears only once and avoidance is not repeated.
